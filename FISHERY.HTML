What is Fishery?

Fishery

A fishery is a location where fish are caught for commercial or recreational purposes. It can be a specific body of water or a collection of fishing activities agreed upon by countries and fishermen. There are often separate fisheries for each target species of fish or shellfish.

Importance of Fishery in the Philippines

Fishery is an important industry in the Philippines, providing both food and income. Here are some of the reasons why fisheries are important in the Philippines:

1. Food Security: Fisheries provide a significant source of protein for Filipinos. Fish and other seafood products are an important part of the Filipino diet, and many households consume them on a regular basis. The fishing industry contributes significantly to the country's food security and ensures that Filipinos have access to affordable and nutritious food.

2. Employment: Fishing is a major contributor to the Philippine economy, contributing 1.6% of GDP in 2020 and providing income to local communities and supporting other industries.

3. Economic Contribution: Fishing is a major contributor to the Philippine economy, contributing 1.6% of GDP in 2020 and providing income to local communities and supporting other industries.

4. Biodiversity and Tourism: The Philippines is known for its rich marine biodiversity, with over 2,400 fish species. The fishing industry contributes to biodiversity conservation and tourism by promoting sustainable fishing practices and conserving marine ecosystems.
