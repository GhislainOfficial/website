What is Forestry?

Forestry

Forestry is the science and art of studying, creating, conserving, using, and managing forests and related processes, organisms that live in them, and all forest-derived resources and products.

Importance of Forestry in the Philippines

Farming is an important sector of the Philippine economy, and it plays an important role in the country's development. Here are some of the reasons why farming is important in the Philippines:

1. Environmental Protection: The Philippines is well-known for its diverse biodiversity and natural resources, and the forestry sector plays an important role in protecting and preserving these resources. Forests help to regulate local climate, reduce erosion, and provide habitat for numerous plant and animal species. They also play an important role in carbon sequestration, which is important for climate change mitigation.

2. Sustain the Environment: Forests support life by providing necessary habitat, food, renewable energy, and materials. They also provide important environmental services as well as spiritual and cultural enrichment opportunities.

3. Economical Growth: Accounting for a sizeable share of the GDP, agriculture is a major driver of the nation's economy. Agricultural items including rice, bananas, and coconuts are significant export commodities, and this is a big factor in driving exports.

4. Cultural Heritage: It provides us with an unbreakable link to the past - to certain social values, beliefs, customs, and traditions that enable us to identify with others and deepen our sense of unity, belonging, and national pride.
